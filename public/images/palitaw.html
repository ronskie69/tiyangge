Palitaw Recipe: Make the Perfectly Chewy and Sweet Filipino Delicacy at Home

Mar 31, 2023 | All Recipes, Dessert | 0 comments

Jump to Recipe Print Recipe

The word “palitaw” comes from the Filipino word “litaw,” which means “to float.” Palitaw is called as such because of the process of cooking the rice dough, which is shaped into balls, flattened and then dropped into boiling water. Once the dough floats to the surface of the water, it is a sign that it is fully cooked and ready to be removed from the pot.

Palitaw a Glutinous Rice Cake

Palitaw is commonly sold in the Philippines as a snack or dessert in local markets, street vendors, and even in some restaurants. It is usually served on a banana leaf and topped with a mixture of grated coconut and sugar. Some vendors may also offer variations of Palitaw such as adding sesame seeds or ground peanuts to the coconut and sugar mixture. In some areas, Palitaw may also be served with a side of sweet syrup made from coconut milk and sugar, adding an extra layer of sweetness to this already delicious treat.

What do you need to make Palitaw?

These ingredients are readily available in most grocery stores or Asian markets. Once you have gathered the necessary ingredients, you will need basic kitchen equipment such as a mixing bowl, a pot for boiling water, a slotted spoon for removing the cooked Palitaw, and a plate or tray for serving.

  • Glutinous rice flour
  • Water
  • Granulated sugar
  • Brown sugar (optional)
  • Sesame Seeds (this elevate the taste)
  • Grated coconut

How to cook Palitaw

Get a pan and toast the sesame seeds until light golden brown. In a separate bowl, mix together the white sugar, brown sugar and the toasted sesame seeds.

In a mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour and water until a dough forms and not sticky. Divide the dough into small pieces and roll them into balls. Flatten each ball to make a small disc shape.

Bring a pot of water to a boil and cook until the Palitaw float to the surface. This should take about 1-2 minutes.

Roll each Palitaw in the grated coconut and sugar mixture until fully coated.

What does Palitaw taste like?

Palitaw has a unique texture and taste. The glutinous rice flour gives it a chewy and sticky texture, while the grated coconut and sugar mixture adds a sweet and nutty flavor.

Overall, Palitaw is a simple yet delicious Filipino delicacy that is loved for its satisfying texture and sweetness. It is a great snack or dessert option that is perfect for any occasion, whether it be a family gathering or a casual afternoon snack.

Palitaw Recipe

Print Recipe Pin Recipe
Prep Time 10 minutes mins
Cook Time 5 minutes mins
Course Dessert
Cuisine Filipino Cuisine
Servings 3

Ingredients
  

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/3 cup white sugar
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • grated coconut
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds

Instructions
 

  • In a low heat, toast the sesame seeds until light golden brown.
  • In a separate bowl, mix together the white sugar, brown sugar and the toasted sesame seeds.
  • In another separate bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour and water until a dough forms. Knead the dough for a few minutes until it becomes smooth and pliable.
  • Divide the dough into small pieces and roll them into balls. Flatten each ball to make a small disc shape.
  • Bring a pot of water to a boil. Carefully drop each flattened ball into the boiling water and cook until they float to the surface. This should take about 1-2 minutes.
  • Using a slotted spoon, remove the Palitaw from the water and place them on a plate or tray.
  • Roll each Palitaw in the grated coconut until fully coated.
  • Roll each Palitaw in the sugar mixture. Optional: Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds.
